Foundations Reflections

Let's reflect on all you have learnt as part of the core skills component of Foundations

10 November 2010

What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the core learning?

  • I don't like to expose myself to other people. Some questions were uncomfortable because I had to remember some painful moments.
  • I don't like to ask for help because I feel that I'm lazy. Because I had some previous knowledge and think that I need to search for some help on the internet first
  • My previous tech knowledge is also holding me back. I want to implement something better and sometimes don't have the patience to do only the basic


What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

I compare the learning process of programming to the process of learning a new language. It requires being aware of ourselves because it's a long journey with frustration, self-doubt and challenging moments. It's common to have negative thoughts, and question if one day we can consider ourselves professionals. Even though we can become professionals, a developer is always to be in the learning mode. Of course, in any career, we need to keep ourselves updated, but working with technology requires more.

What has surprised you the most about the core learning?
What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

The topics are really important, especially to reflect on our weaknesses and dealing with frustration. I need to change my routine and pay attention to moments of self-awareness and reflection to fight against my anxiety. However, I have to confess the way to work with these topics didn't work for me. I don't like to expose myself and also it's a content a bit boring to read. I would like to learn and practice them using other activities.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

Because of the difficulties that learning programming brings to the students. I know how it can be challenging and make people think they're stupid and suffer from impostor syndrome. I see the value, but I would like to spend time with this in another way. Exposing this in the blog didn't help me. I hope that during the Bootcamp we can work on this in another way.

Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead? Justify your answer.

I need to confess that I would like to spend more time learning tech skills, but again it's more due to the way that we had to work on the core material. Also, the mix of this content with the technical part, makes me to skip this part which is necessary to the course. I had to complete the pages with lorem ipsum to feel more comfortable and complete the blog structure.
