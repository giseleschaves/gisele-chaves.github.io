12 February 2024

1 - Explain a situation where you have made an ethical decision. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and how you reflect on the decision now.

Years ago I was working as tech lead in a company, and one of my coworkers asked for a salary raising. She was an awesome recourse but I didn't have ownership to decide it, so I pass it thorught the manager.

Unfortunately the company didn't have budget to raise her salary and I had to give her this answer. She of course was dissapointed and weeks later she resign, saying that she found another job and ask only for one week of notice. We were under a lot of pression due to a project, but it's the risk the company decided to take. We found another person and everyhting looked great.

At the last day of her work, she decided to talk directly with the manager saying that she regret and didn't want to leave the job. The manager decided to ask me to join the meeting to know my opinion in the case. I had to explain to her that was really sad, but we couldn't leave the other person who also was in his notice period. It was not fair with him.

What makes this situation much more complex is, she was my best friend. We met each other there and she told me that she didn't have any other offer. But asked me to keep this as secrect. Despite my many advices to her for not doing this, ask for leaving laying about a new offer. She decided this was the only way to have a salary raising. I had a really difficult decision, and unfortunatelly she never forgive me. :-(


2 - Describe how your culture and the people around you have influenced your values and identity.

I grow up in Brazil and our work culture is really different then in New Zealand. The two main differences the concept abot work life balance and feedbak.

  • Work life balance - In my culture the social inequality makes people have the work as the most important thing in their life. Because of this, it's expected to work much more hours and sacrifyce your personal life.
  • Feedback - It's not commom to give a honest and direct feedback in my culture. People often take things too personal or don't give constructive feedback to develop and growth others.

    • Working as a manager I always try to work in these values. I had a manager from German who helped me a lot to understand feedback
